Yellowface

by R.F. Kuang

My redesign of Yellowface captures the novel's themes of deception, cultural appropriation, and identity. A typewriter and manuscript take center stage, smudged with fingerprints, symbolizing stolen words. Shadows and a dimly lit window hint at secrecy, while fragmented Asian eyes subtly expose the false identity June adopts. Layers of peeling paper represent the unraveling of her deception, and a scattered pancake adds eerie irony. This cover challenges viewers to questionâ€”what is real, and what is stolen?
